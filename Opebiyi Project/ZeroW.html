OUR MISSION

" Our mission is to inspire and empower everyone to take action against food waste. - to contribute in every way we can to building the global food waste movement. It's only when we all come together to fight food waste, that we'll be able to generate a positive change in society."

47 million tons of food gets wasted in Africa households every year, which is more than half of the total food waste in the Continent. We believe that raising awareness about the issue and how to help solve it are the keys to success.
The issue of food waste can feel overwhelming but even the smallest changes in our daily habits can make a difference.

Tips to to avoid food waste in your Home

Plan ahead and get creative

Planning your meals, making a shopping list and not overbuying while in-store can drastically reduce your food waste at home.
Handy tricks to plan ahead
  • Do your inventory;Check what food you have left in the fridge and cupboards
  • Plan your shopping; plan out your meals, as much as you can, and make a shopping list that you can use in store so you don't end up buying anything unnecessary.
  • Freeze freeze leftovers for later, both in one big portion or separated into smaller portions for single servings.
  • Get inspiration for meals There are so many great recipes available on the internet and in cook books. Check what needs to be eaten first, get creative with what you have in your kitchen and you'll definitely be able to rustle up something delicious.
  • Do weekly leftovers meals Weeks worth of leftovers can often end up being enough food for a full meal. Get creative with the week's leftovers and experiment with new dishes

Store Food Properly

Knowing what you have, where you have it and how to store it is undoubtedly the smartest way to reduce food-waste.
Tropical and subtropical food such as tomatoes, melons, eggplants, bananas, peppers should be kept at room temperature
Store with refrigeration; Adopt the First In First Out rotation method by placing newer items at the back of the fridge and older ones at the front try to leave enough space so that air can circulate to all parts of the fridge
Store by freezing; Freeze your leftovers and the fresh items you know you won’t eat in the following days. It extends the life of food, as long as it’s done before the use-by date, by preventing micro-organisms from growing and slowing down the enzyme activity that causes food to spoil.

Take advantage of technology

You don't have to look far to be able to reduce food waste in your day to day life. A lot of nifty things are out there to make you and your kitchen ready to fight food waste. Invest in a dehydrator to make delicious and long-lasting snacks out of fruits and veggies or get that blender out of the cupboard to make smoothies and soups. A juicer is also an easy and healthy way to avoid throwing out overripe fruit and veg. Try to keep an eye out for the new technologies available that make it easier to help save the planet.
